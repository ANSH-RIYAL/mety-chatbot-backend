# How to Get Your $300 Refund from Google Cloud

## Immediate Actions

### 1. Contact Google Cloud Support (DO THIS NOW)

**Link:** https://cloud.google.com/support

**Steps:**
1. Click "Contact Support" or "Create Case"
2. Select issue type: **"Billing"**
3. Priority: **"P1 - Critical"** (you're being charged unexpectedly)
4. Subject: **"Unexpected $300 charge despite free credits promise - Request refund"**

**Message Template:**
```
I am requesting a full refund of $300 in unexpected charges from project srs-creator-app.

SITUATION:
- I was told by Google Cloud that charges would not exceed my free credits
- I received $XXX in free credits (or was on free tier)
- Despite this promise, I was charged $300 without any notification
- I did not receive billing alerts or warnings before the charge
- The charge occurred on [DATE - check your billing statement]

PROJECT DETAILS:
- Project ID: srs-creator-app
- Project Number: 738553634957
- Billing Account: [Your billing account]

REQUEST:
I request a full refund of $300 due to:
1. Misleading information about free credits policy
2. Lack of billing alerts/warnings
3. No notification before charges exceeded credits
4. I was unable to monitor or control costs as promised

I understand Google Cloud's billing policies, but I was explicitly told charges would not exceed my credits, which was not the case.

Please process a full refund and help me understand how to prevent this in the future.
```

### 2. Check Your Billing Statement

**Link:** https://console.cloud.google.com/billing

**What to look for:**
- When the charges occurred
- What services were charged
- If there were any free credits applied
- If billing alerts were sent (check email)

### 3. Document Everything

Take screenshots of:
- Billing statement showing the $300 charge
- Any emails from Google about free credits
- Any promises about charges not exceeding credits
- Your billing alerts settings (or lack thereof)

### 4. Escalate if Needed

If initial support doesn't help:
- Ask to speak to a billing specialist
- Request escalation to a manager
- Mention you're considering filing a complaint with your credit card company
- Reference Google's customer service commitments

## Why You Should Get a Refund

1. **Misleading Information**: If Google told you charges wouldn't exceed credits, that's a promise
2. **No Alerts**: You should have received billing alerts before charges
3. **Lack of Control**: You couldn't monitor or stop the charges
4. **Good Faith**: You're a customer trying to use free tier/credits appropriately

## If Google Refuses

1. **Dispute with Credit Card**: File a chargeback for "services not as described"
2. **File Complaint**: 
   - Better Business Bureau
   - Your local consumer protection agency
3. **Document Everything**: Keep all emails and screenshots
